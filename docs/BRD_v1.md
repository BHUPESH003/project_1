1\. Executive Summary
---------------------

The product is a **local commerce coordination platform** that enables users to order from nearby local sellers and get items delivered through **aggregated third‑party delivery providers**, without calls, follow‑ups, or manual coordination.

The MVP focuses on **Printing** as the first live category, while the system is designed to be **category‑agnostic** to support future expansion (stationery, hardware, pick & drop, etc.).

* * *

2\. Problem Statement
---------------------

### User Problems

*   Users need **urgent local items/services**.
    
*   They currently rely on:
    
    *   Physical visits
        
    *   Phone calls
        
    *   WhatsApp coordination
        
    *   Separately booking delivery
        
*   This results in:
    
    *   Time waste
        
    *   Uncertainty
        
    *   Failed or delayed fulfillment
        

### Seller Problems

*   Local sellers (print shops, etc.) operate **100% walk‑in**.
    
*   No delivery capability.
    
*   No digital order intake.
    
*   Miss out on demand that expects doorstep delivery.
    

#### Seller Availability

*   Local sellers are not available 24/7, leading to potential missed orders during off-hours.
    

### Market Gap

There is **no single system** that:

*   Coordinates **order + seller + delivery**
    
*   Works across **multiple local categories**
    
*   Does **not own inventory or fleet**
    

* * *

3\. Business Objective
----------------------

### Primary Objective (North Star)

*   **Increase Daily Completed Orders (DCO)**
    

### Secondary Objectives

*   High order success rate
    
*   Low manual intervention
    
*   Seller acceptance reliability
    

* * *

4\. Target Users
----------------

### Primary User Personas

1.  **Individual consumers**
    
    *   Urgent local needs
        
    *   Willing to pay delivery fee
        
2.  **Office admins**
    
    *   Frequent printing needs
        
    *   Time‑sensitive
        

### Seller Personas

*   Local print shops (initial)
    
*   Digitally comfortable
    
*   No in‑house delivery
    

* * *

5\. In Scope (MVP v1)
---------------------

### Business Scope

*   City: **Gurgaon**
    
*   Launch mode: **Semi‑open (limited area)**
    
*   Live category: **Printing**
    

### Platform Capabilities

*   User app (order creation)
    
*   Seller app (order fulfillment)
    
*   Delivery via **third‑party aggregators**
    
*   Platform‑issued invoice (non‑delivery)
    
*   Manual admin intervention allowed
    
*   Sellers can mark themselves Online or Offline
    
*   Orders are routed only to Online sellers
    

* * *

6\. Out of Scope (Explicitly)
-----------------------------

*   Owning inventory
    
*   Owning delivery fleet
    
*   Discounts / promotions
    
*   Subscriptions
    
*   Ratings & reviews
    
*   Food / grocery in MVP
    
*   Public city‑wide launch
    

* * *

7\. Value Proposition
---------------------

### For Users

*   One app
    
*   One flow
    
*   Guaranteed coordination
    
*   No calls, no follow‑ups
    

### For Sellers

*   New orders
    
*   No delivery management
    
*   No tech complexity
    
*   Commission only on success
    

### For Platform

*   Asset‑light
    
*   Scalable across categories
    
*   Low operational burn
    

* * *

8\. Revenue Model
-----------------

### User Pays

*   Item price
    
*   Delivery fee (explicit, not subsidized)
    

### Seller Pays

*   Commission: **5–8%** (category‑dependent)
    

### Delivery Partner

*   Paid delivery fee
    
*   Optional platform commission (later)
    

* * *

9\. Key Assumptions (Validated / Unvalidated)
---------------------------------------------

### Validated

*   Users are willing to pay delivery fees
    
*   Sellers have sufficient margins
    
*   Printing has urgency and repeat behavior
    

### Unvalidated (To Monitor)

*   Seller response time consistency
    
*   Aggregator pricing stability
    
*   Multi‑category demand overlap
    

* * *

10\. Dependencies & Risks
-------------------------

### Dependencies

*   Availability of delivery aggregators
    
*   Seller order acceptance discipline
    

### Key Risks

*   Delivery cost spikes
    
*   Seller delays hurting UX
    
*   Aggregator entering marketplace directly
    
*   Incorrect availability state can impact order success
    

* * *

11\. Success Criteria (MVP)
---------------------------

The MVP is successful if:

*   Daily completed orders show **week‑on‑week growth**
    
*   Majority of orders complete without manual escalation
    
*   Sellers consistently accept and fulfill orders
    

* * *

12\. Strategic Direction (Post‑MVP)
-----------------------------------

*   Expand categories on same rails
    
*   Increase seller density
    
*   Improve delivery cost optimization
    
*   Reduce manual ops gradually
    

* * *

13\. Guiding Principles (Non‑Negotiable)
----------------------------------------

*   Category‑agnostic architecture
    
*   Separate User & Seller apps
    
*   Coordination first, not speed wars
    
*   Clarity over feature richness
    
*   Ops before automation
